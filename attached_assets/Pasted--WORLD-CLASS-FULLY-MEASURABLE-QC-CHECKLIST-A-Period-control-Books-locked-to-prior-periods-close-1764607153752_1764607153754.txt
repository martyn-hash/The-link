✅ WORLD-CLASS, FULLY MEASURABLE QC CHECKLIST
A. Period control

Books locked to prior periods

close_date >= end_of_previous_period.

No back-dated transactions

No transactions with TxnDate < close_date.

No excessively future-dated transactions

No transactions with TxnDate > period_end + 30 days.

B. Bank & cash

All bank accounts reconciled to period end

Reconciliation report exists for period_end.

Ending difference = £0.

GL balance matches online bank balance

|gl_balance – online_feed_balance| <= £1.

Unreconciled bank items below threshold

< 3 unreconciled items OR

< £50 total unreconciled.

Undeposited funds cleared

Undeposited Funds account balance = £0 ± £5.

C. Sales & accounts receivable

No missing invoice numbers or gaps

Invoice numbers sequential, or gaps flagged for approval.

Debtors ageing within tolerance

No invoices > 120 days old unless manually approved.

60-day invoices represent < X% of total (e.g., 20%).

No negative debtor balances

No customer has a negative AR balance beyond -£5.

Matched receipts

Bank feed deposits allocated to invoices unless “Other Income” is appropriate.

Unmatched receipts < X% of monthly sales (e.g., <5%).

D. Purchases, AP & expenses

No old unpaid bills without approval

Bills >120 days old flagged unless noted.

60-day bills represent < X% of total liabilities.

No negative supplier balances

No supplier shows < -£5 balance.

Large expenses coded correctly

Any transaction >£500 matches expected COA/VAT rules.

Outliers flagged.

Misc/other/suspense postings minimal

No single uncategorised/misc/suspense entry > £50.

Total misc/suspense < £100.

E. VAT & indirect taxes

Correct VAT code assignment by category

Insurance = exempt

Wages = no VAT

Bank charges = exempt

Fuel = 20% or partial (configurable)

Others follow expected rules

Zero VAT exceptions

No transactions in VAT Exception report (or all justified & approved).

VAT return reconciles to general ledger

VAT control balance matches return total ±£1.

Variance vs. prior quarter within range

Output VAT variance < ±25% (unless seasonal).

Input VAT variance < ±25%.

Any variance > threshold requires approval note.

F. Journals & general ledger

Payroll journals present

At least 1 payroll journal in period.

Contains lines for wages, employer NI, etc.

Depreciation journals done (if FA exists)

If fixed assets > £1,000: depreciation journal present monthly/quarterly.

No manual journals to restricted accounts

No JE posted to:

VAT control

Sales/revenue accounts

Directors Loan control

Bank accounts
without an approval flag.

Suspense and AMA (Ask My Accountant) = £0

Both accounts balance = £0 ±£5.

G. Attachments & audit trail

Attachments present where required

All expenses >£50 must have attachment or bill.

All supplier bills must have attachment.

Exceptions flagged.

All bank feed transactions processed

No bank transactions older than X days left unprocessed (e.g., 7).

H. Master data

New suppliers/customers approved

All created this period flagged unless reviewed.

No duplicate suppliers/customers

Similar names/emails/phone flagged.

New COA accounts reviewed/approved

Any account created this period must be explicitly approved.

I. Analytical, quantifiable “sanity tests”

Turnover trend within expected band

Revenue within ±25% of average of last 3 comparable months.

Or seasonal pattern rule applied.

Gross margin within expected band

GM% within ±10% of prior 3 months average.

If outside → approval note required.

Operating expense spikes detected

Categories with >200% spike flagged for review.

Or absolute variance > £X (e.g., £1,000).

Directors Loan Account movement reconciled

Net DLA movement must be fully explained by:

dividends

payroll

drawings

transfers

expense claims

Unexplained movement < £50.
(Fully codifiable: sum of expected categories must reconcile to movement.)

Profit variance check

Net profit variance vs prior 3-month average within ±30%.

Or flagged for explanation.
